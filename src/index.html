About Me

I am a mathematician and computer scientist currently working as a research assistant for Professor Jorge Tamayo at Harvard Business School. My research focuses on microeconomics and organizational economics, where I explore firm behavior and strategic decision-making in multiunit organizations through the lens of game theory and dynamic optimization.

With a strong foundation in mathematics and computer science from Universidad Nacional de Colombia, I blend theoretical modeling with computational methods to analyze complex economic scenarios. My technical expertise extends to artificial intelligence and machine learning, which I leverage to enhance and validate theoretical predictions through large-scale numerical simulations.
